Chris Paulik

1999-Present

Mechanical

College you attended, degree earned, and major/current work experience.

I attended the University of Wisconsin-Madison and received a Bachelors of Science in Mechanical Engineering. I immediately started working for GE Healthcare (then GE Medical Systems) in 1999 as a design engineer. I have moved around GE Healthcare in various roles within the engineering department, and now work within the Regulatory Affairs group working with global regulators to get our products approved in all of the countries around the globe.

Why did you become a mentor for team 537?

As a young engineer at GE Healthcare, I was invited to learn about a program called FIRST while they fed those that attended free pizza. I went for the pizza, and fell in love with the program. I offered up my Alma Mater, Sussex-Hamilton High School, as our flagship school to try this FIRST thing out. The rest is history.

What is your favorite thing about being a mentor for team 537?

My favorite thing about mentoring for FIRST is seeing the excitement the students show when they see for the first time something they created work on the field. Also, to see students that I have mentored come back with successful jobs, and decide to share the knowledge they have learned with a new generation of students by becoming mentors themselves.

Why do you continue to mentor our team?

FIRST is the only program I know of that truly embodies everything a company does and experiences to release a new product. It is wonderful for students to get real world experience before even entering college.

Dave Woods

2015 - Present

College you attended, degree earned, and major/current work experience.

I graduated from Milwaukee School of Engineering with a Bachelor of Science (B.S.) Degree in Mechanical Engineering; with a focus on robots designed for excavation and mining operations in space. I am currently the Lead Mechanical Engineer under the Engineering and Innovation group of Rexnord Corporation; where I spend the majority of my time developing new Industrial Chain and Conveying Equipment products for (primarily) the mining and bulk material handling industries.

Why did you become a mentor for team 537?

My experience as a student on FRC#0537 and as a Co-Lead Mentor of FRC#2506 gave me a significant advantage in college and my professional career. I believe it is my duty to continue paying-it-forward and provide the next generation of students with the best practices and learnings from my experience so that they can develop their technical and leadership talent required to accelerate their future careers, provide our sponsors with superior employees, and supply our community with more engaged citizens.

What is your favorite thing about being a mentor for team 537?

Compared to other FIRST Robotics teams I have mentored, FRC#0537 has established the most resources (facilities, number of students, number of mentors, 2 to 3 times the financial sponsorship, etc.) and the strongest community involvement; which should enable the team to execute more interesting & difficult activities and events. While FRC#0537 has a history of success in numerous areas, the students and mentors have identified some notable gaps and areas of improvement that align with my current skillset.

Why do you continue to mentor our team?

Ultimately, the end product is the experience we provide our students, and the technical and leadership talent they gain in the process. I feel it is important to provide students with tools and methodologies they can use to solve future challenges in their lives and careers; such that they become better employees for our corporate partners and sponsoring businesses, and more engaged citizens within our community.
